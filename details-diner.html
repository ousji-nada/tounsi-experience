The Experience

Where History Becomes Dinner

Step through an unassuming wooden door in the heart of Tunis' UNESCO-listed medina and find yourself transported into another century. A 17th-century riad courtyard — tilework, jasmine, the sound of an oud drifting from somewhere you can't quite locate — awaits you as your private dining room for the evening.

Your host, Hédi, descends from a family of master cooks whose recipes predate the Ottoman era. His slow-cooked lamb with preserved lemon and ras el hanout has been passed through four generations without a single written recipe. Tonight, he shares it with you.

The menu unfolds over five courses: a warm kemia spread of roasted peppers and olive tapenade, silky harissa-spiced soup, the legendary lamb tagine, a selection of Tunisian sweets dripping with orange blossom water, and finishing with the ceremonial three-pour mint tea. Every ingredient is sourced from the same souk Hédi's grandmother visited each morning.

A local oud player provides the soundtrack. The night sky above the open courtyard provides the ceiling. You provide nothing but your appetite and curiosity.

What's Included
  • Welcome Aperitif Chilled local wine or pomegranate juice served on arrival
  • 5-Course Tunisian Feast Full menu from kemia to dessert, including the signature slow-cooked lamb
  • Live Oud Performance Traditional Malouf music performed throughout the evening
  • Ceremonial Mint Tea Ritual The famous three-pour tea ceremony to close the evening
  • Guided Photo Moments Hédi takes you through the most photogenic corners of his medina
  • Take-Home Spice Mix A small bag of Hédi's personal ras el hanout blend to recreate the magic at home

Not included: Transport to/from the Medina, additional alcoholic beverages beyond the welcome drink, tips (appreciated but never expected).
