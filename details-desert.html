The Experience

Where the Earth
Ends and the Sky Begins

There is a particular silence that exists only in the Sahara. It is not the absence of sound — it is the presence of everything you've been too busy to hear. This overnight camp experience begins in Douz, the gateway to the Great Eastern Erg, where your guide Ahmed — a third-generation Bedouin whose family has crossed these dunes for centuries — will be waiting.

You mount your camel at dusk and ride for 90 minutes into the desert as the sky turns shades of orange and violet that no camera has ever fully captured. The camp is a cluster of traditional Berber tents arranged in a horseshoe around a central fire, furnished with hand-woven rugs, lanterns, and cushions. Dinner is a slow-cooked mechoui lamb buried in sand-pit embers, served with dates, flatbread, and cardamom tea beneath a sky so thick with stars it feels low enough to touch.

You sleep in the silence. You wake before the horizon decides to light up, and when it does — from behind a wall of perfectly curved dunes — you understand completely why humans have been crossing this desert for thousands of years. The tea is already brewing. The bread is already warm. Ahmed is already smiling.

What to Prepare

Desert Temperatures

Days can reach 40°C; nights drop to 12°C. Bring layers — we provide extra blankets.

What to Bring

Small backpack, sunscreen, sunglasses, a light jacket, and a sense of wonder. Leave suitcases behind.

Connectivity

No WiFi in the desert. Mobile signal is minimal. This is intentional and wonderful.

Fitness Level

Suitable for all fitness levels. The camel trek is gentle. Alternative 4x4 transfer available.
